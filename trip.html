📌 Two things still to handle

① Disney tickets

Tokyo Disneyland (recommended over DisneySea for a 4yo) — tickets are date-stamped and dynamically priced.

  • Recommended day: Friday Jun 12 (off-peak vs Saturday)
  • Buy: 1 Adult + 2 Child (4–11). Katherine (4) is the youngest paid tier; under-4 would be free.
  • Cost estimate: ¥7,900–10,900 adult + 2 × ¥4,700–5,600 child = ~¥17,300–22,100 (~$115–148)
  • Where: tokyodisneyresort.jp/en/ticket — MyDisney account required, tickets become QR codes in the app.
  • When to buy: 1–3 days ahead is fine. Don’t buy now; cheaper to wait and see weather/energy. They sell out only on holidays/weekends, not weekday June.
Just-decide-now option: buy Fri Jun 12 Disneyland 1-Day Passport × (1 adult + 2 child) on the evening of Jun 10 once you’ve seen the weather and the kids’ recovery from jetlag.

② Commute card (Suica / Pasmo)

Yes, get one — flat-rate trains, buses, vending machines, conbini all tap-to-pay. Saves the “buy individual tickets with two kids in tow” pain.

  • Easiest (zero physical card): Add Mobile Suica to iPhone Wallet before you leave SFO — Wallet → + → Transit Card → Suica → load any ¥ amount with Apple Pay. Tap your phone at the gate.
  • Kids: kids 6+ ride at child fare (Andy). Under 6 (Katherine) is free with an adult. Mobile Suica doesn’t support child accounts — just buy Andy a paper child ticket at the machine (or skip — short rides are ¥150, not worth the friction).
  • Backup paper option: grab a Welcome Suica at NRT (JR East Travel Service Center, arrivals) — no ¥500 deposit, valid 28 days. Perfect for a kid to have their own.
  • Realistic load: ¥3,000 per card to start; top up at any station.
Just-decide-now option: Mobile Suica on your iPhone tonight (5 min, no Japan needed). At NRT, grab one Welcome Suica for Andy so he feels official. Skip a card for Katherine.

📶 Data plan — pick one

You go Japan → China. China blocks Google, Maps, Gmail, WhatsApp without a workaround — so a plan that handles both legs wins. Most "Japan eSIM" articles miss this for your situation.

🏆 Recommended for this trip: Google Fi. One plan covers both countries, and in China it tunnels through Google's network — so Gmail, Maps, YouTube just work, with no separate VPN to install or troubleshoot at 4am with two jet-lagged kids. Add a line online (~$50 for the month), use eSIM activation, cancel after the trip. iPhone-friendly.
